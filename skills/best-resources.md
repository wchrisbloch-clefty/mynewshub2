# Best Learning Resources Finder

Top 5 only. Quality over quantity. Web search required — never recommend from memory alone.

## Step 1 — Define the Search

Establish:
- **Topic**: [What needs to be learned]
- **Format preference**: [Book / video / course / person / all]
- **Time constraint**: [Quick overview vs. deep mastery]
- **CB's starting level**: [Beginner / intermediate / advanced]
- **Purpose**: [Decision-making / BD prep / investment thesis / skill building]

## Step 2 — Search Protocol

Run targeted searches before recommending:

1. "[topic] best book [current year]" — find what practitioners recommend, not just bestsellers
2. "[topic] best course online [current year]" — check Coursera, Udemy, Masterclass, YouTube
3. "[topic] experts to follow" — LinkedIn, Twitter/X, Substack
4. "[topic] best podcast episode" — find the definitive 1-2 hour listen
5. "[topic] CB context" — search for energy/finance/BD-specific applications

Flag: publication date, author credibility, estimated time investment, and why this source specifically.

|---|---|---|---|---|---|

**Ranking criteria:**
- Practitioner > academic (someone who did it > someone who studied it)
- Specific > general (energy finance > general finance)
- Current > classic (unless the classic is genuinely foundational)
- CB's circle of competence first (energy, power, Gulf Coast, ERCOT, income investing)

## Step 4 — The Curation Rule

Per CB's operating principle: **Curation > Adoption.**

After finding all sources, filter to exactly 5. If a source doesn't earn its slot, cut it.

Flag: "These 5 cover 80% of what you need. Adding more inputs without reason = drift."

## Hard Rules
- Never recommend without searching — stale recommendations waste CB's most scarce resource (time)
- Never recommend more than 5 — curation is the skill
- Always flag the CB angle — a resource without a specific connection to CB's context is lower priority
- Keep this separate from AI Pulse (Wolfe/Anthropic/Fridman) — AI Pulse is curated and locked; this is topic-specific and on-demand
